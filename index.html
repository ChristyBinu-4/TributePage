Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India

Here's a time line of Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam's life:

  • 1931 - Born in Rameswaram, Madras Presidency, British India(present-day Tamil Nadu, India) on 15 October 1931. His father Jainulabdeen Marakayar was a boat owner and imam of a local mosque; his mother Ashiamma was a housewife. He has 3 brothers and 1 sister. Among them APJ Abdul Kalam was the youngest of all the children in the family.
  • 1954 - He completed his education at the Schwartz Higher Secondary School,Ramanthapuram. He done his graduation study in St.Joseph's College, Thiruchirappalli, then affiliated with the University of Madras
  • 1955 - He Studied Aerospace Engineering in Madras Institute of Technology. His dream is to become a fighter pilot. He missed it because he achieved the ninth position but only eight position were allowed in IAF.
  • 1960 - APJ Abdul Kalam designed a small hovercraft at DRDO, while working with the Indian National Committe for Space Research (INCOSPAR) under the space scientist Vikram Sarabhai.
  • 1963 - He visited NASA
  • 1965 - Kalam started working at DRDO for the expandable rocket project independently.
  • 1969 - He was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) from DRDO in 1969. He was the project directot of India's Launch Vehicle(SLV III).
  • 1970 - Kalam directed two projects named Project Devil and project valiant which sought to develop ballistic missiles from the technology of the successfull SLV programme.
  • 1981 - He was honored by Government of India by giving the Padma Bhushan award for his excellancy.
  • 1982 - He rejoined DRDO and planned the program that produced a number of successful missiles, which helped earn him the nickname "Missile Man". Among those successes was Agni, India's first intermediate-range ballistic missile.
  • 1989 - SLV III was launched.
  • 1990 - He was honored by Government of India by giving Padma vibhushan award for his excellancy.
  • 1992 - Abdul Kalam was scientific adviser to the defense minister, and he later served as principal adviser from 1992 to 1999. He played a political technological role for pokhran II during this period.
  • 1994 - Institute of Directors honored him with Distinguished Fellow Award.
  • 1995 - National Academy of Sciences Honored him with Honorary Fellow award.
  • 1997 - He was honored with Bharath Ratna award and Indira Gandhi Award for National Integration.
  • 1998 - Veer Savarkar Award was given him from Indian government
  • 2000 - Received Ramanujan Award from Alwars Research Center Chennai
  • 2002 - The Missile man won the Presidential election. During the period of 2002 to 2007 he put forward a countrywide plan called Technology Vision 2020, which he described as a road map for transforming India from a less-developed to a developed society in 20 years. The plan called for, among other measures, increasing agricultural productivity, emphasizing technology as a vehicle for economic growth, and widening access to health care and education.
  • 2007 - He was honoured with Honorary Doctorate of Science award by University of Wolverhampton, UK. He received an award name King Charles II Medal , by Royal Society U.K. Honorary Doctorate of Science and Technology, carnegie mellon University.
  • 2008 - He received lots of awards in this year for doctorate of science, Aligarh Muslim University. Doctor of Engineering (Honoris Causa) Nanyang Technological University, Singapore. Doctor of Science University Sains Malaysia.
  • 2011 After completing his President post he became a visiting professor for Indian Institute of Management shillong, Ahmedabad, Banglore, Indore.
  • 2012 Abdul Kalam launched a programme for the youth of India called the What Can I Give Movement, with a central theme of defeating corruption.
  • 2015 He travelled to Shillong to make a lecture about the "Creating a Livable Planet Earth". He passed away from us while climbing the stairs. His death is due to sudden cardiac arrest.
"I worked very closely with Dr. Kalam as PM and I greatly benefitted from his advice as Pres of our country. His life and work will be remembered for generations to come" -- Indian Prime Minister Manmohan Singh
